# Screenshot Guide for Final Presentation

When submitting your final report or creating a presentation slide deck, use the following guidelines to capture the best angles of the Smart Study Planner.

**Tip:** Before taking screenshots, press `F11` to go full screen and hide your browser tabs. Ensure your browser zoom is at 100%.

## 1. The Main Dashboard (Light & Dark Mode)
- **What to capture:** The overview cards, the "Today's Schedule" timeline, and the Exam Countdown widget.
- **Why:** Shows the clean UI and the breadth of integrated data.
- **Bonus:** Take one screenshot in Light mode, then click the toggle and take the exact same screenshot in Dark Mode to show off theming support.

## 2. Analytics Radar & Heatmap
- **What to capture:** Navigate to the Analytics page. Ensure you have at least 3 subjects with logged data so the **Radar Chart** is visible. Capture the Activity Heatmap and the AI Insights box.
- **Why:** Data visualization always scores high in academic/technical evaluations.

## 3. The RAG AI "Chat with Notes"
- **What to capture:** A split view or sequential screenshots.
  1. The **Library** page showing a specific PDF uploaded.
  2. The **AI Assistant** page with the "Chat with Notes" toggle engaged. Show the AI giving a specific answer that explicitly says "Based on your uploaded notes...".
- **Why:** Proves the LLM integration is not just a basic wrapper, but actually uses local vector context.

## 4. The Pomodoro Focus Space
- **What to capture:** Enter "Focus Mode" (the full screen, giant timer). Let the timer run down a bit so the SVG progress ring is visible. 
- **Why:** Visually striking and highlights the calming UI/UX principles of the project.

## 5. Gamification Badges
- **What to capture:** The Achievements page showing the circular Level Progress bar and the unlocked Badges.
- **Why:** Shows the database relationships (activity logs triggering XP calculation).
